A

bout the Market

The Lienzer Adventmarkt, often called “Christkindlmarkt”, is a cherished tradition in Tyrol.
The name “Christkindlmarkt” refers to the belief that on Christmas eve, the Christkind (Baby Jesus) comes to town and gives presents to the children.

First organized in 1997, it has grown to feature 35 beautifully decorated stands.
The market offers traditional crafts and delicious regional treats like gingerbread and mulled wine.

Accompanied by festive music and lights, the event hosts around 60 activities each year, making it a magical destination for families and visitors alike.

C

raft your Christmas presents yourself

The "Living Workshop" at the Lienz Advent Market offers visitors a chance to watch artisans and artists demonstrate their crafts in real time. Guests can observe or even participate in activities.

Some Highlights:

Visitors can learn about the mystical world of herbs, wool products and felting techniques, soap-making, crafting greeting cards, making dolls, and creating Christmas ornaments, with artisans explaining each step of their process.

L

earn about Lienz' History

Lienz' Night Watchman

The multilingual night watchman Hannes Rohracher is known for sharing historical insights about the city and enjoying chatting with visitors.

At nightfall, armed with a lantern and halberd, he patrols the market, ensuring all is in order. At precisely 9:00 PM, his traditional night call signals the end of the market day.

S

ocial organizations

Be good, do good.

At the 28th Lienz Advent Market, seven associations participate that support social and humanitarian projects, primarily for those in need in the Lienz district and occasionally abroad.

The participating organizations and their dates include:

  • Hand in Hand: Nov 22–24
  • Ausbildungsfit Osttirol: Nov 25–28
  • Diakonie de La Tour: Dec 2–5
  • Rotary Club Lienz: Dec 6–8
  • SoLaLi: Dec 9–12
  • ÖZIV: Dec 13–15
  • Bildung bringt Frieden: Dec 16–23

O

ther Activities

The Adventmarkt in Lienz also offers winterly activities like an Ice Stock Tournament where you can compete for a 1000€ prize in local vouchers.

The kids have the possibility to show their skills at ice skating, skates can be borrowed free of charge at Advent Stand 19 during market hours.

Families can enjoy a range of activities in a heated pavilion from 3–7 PM, including crafts, decorating gingerbread, singing, writing letters, and more.
